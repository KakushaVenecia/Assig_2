CSS controls the fonts, text, colors, backgrounds, margins, and layout. It gives a website its look and layout .

CSS enables us to:

  • Specify fonts other than the default for the browser
  • Specify color and size of text and links
  • Apply colors to backgrounds
  • Contain webpage elements in boxes and float those boxes to specific positions on the page

In CSS, a margin is the space around an element's border, while padding is the space between an element's border and the element's content . Put another way, the margin property controls the space outside an element, and the padding property controls the space inside an element. Border is the strip that goes around your div/element of the content. In general, use margins when you're adjusting the spacing of an element in relation to another element (i.e a div in relation to another div on the page), and padding when you're adjusting the look of an individual element (i.e the amount of pixels between the edge of a div and the text within it).
